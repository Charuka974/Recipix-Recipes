Ingredients:

For the Pie Crust:

  2. 1 tbsp sugar
  3. 1 tsp salt
  4. 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  5. ¬º cup ice water (more if needed)

For the Apple Filling:

  1. 6-7 medium apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp work well)
  2. ¬æ cup sugar
  3. 1 tsp cinnamon
  4. 1 tbsp lemon juice
  5. 2 tbsp flour (for thickening)
  6. 1 tbsp butter (for dotting)

Instructions:

Prepare Crust: In a bowl, mix flour, sugar, and salt. Add chilled butter cubes and cut them into the flour using a pastry cutter or fork until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add ice water, a tablespoon at a time, mixing until the dough just comes together. Divide the dough into two halves, wrap in plastic wrap, and chill in the fridge for at least 1 hour.
Prepare Filling: Peel, core, and slice the apples. In a separate bowl, mix apples with sugar, cinnamon, lemon juice, and flour until evenly coated.
Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 425¬∞F (220¬∞C).
Assemble the Pie: Roll out one half of the dough on a floured surface into a circle large enough to fit a 9-inch pie dish. Place the dough in the dish, then fill with the apple mixture. Dot the top of the filling with small pieces of butter.
Add Top Crust: Roll out the second half of the dough and place it over the apples. Trim excess dough, then crimp the edges to seal the pie. Cut small slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.
Bake: Place the pie on a baking sheet (to catch any drips) and bake at 425¬∞F (220¬∞C) for 45-50 minutes, until the crust is golden and the filling is bubbly. If the crust starts to get too dark, cover the edges with foil.
Cool: Let the pie cool for at least 2 hours before slicing to allow the filling to set.

22. Japchae (Korean Stir-Fried Glass Noodles) üçú

Ingredients:

  • 200g sweet potato starch noodles (dangmyeon)
  • 150g beef (sirloin or rib-eye), thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 bell pepper (red or green), julienned
  • 100g spinach, blanched
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil (for stir-frying)
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

Cook the Noodles: Bring a pot of water to boil, add the sweet potato starch noodles, and cook for 6-8 minutes until tender. Drain and rinse with cold water. Cut the noodles into shorter lengths, if necessary.
Cook the Beef: In a pan, heat a little vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the thinly sliced beef, season with 1 tbsp soy sauce, and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until browned. Remove and set aside.
Stir-Fry Vegetables: In the same pan, add a little more oil and saut√© garlic, onion, carrots, and bell pepper until softened (about 3-4 minutes). Remove the vegetables and set aside.
Cook Spinach: Blanch the spinach in boiling water for 1-2 minutes, then squeeze out excess water and season with 1 tsp soy sauce and a little sesame oil.
Combine and Stir-Fry: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked noodles, beef, vegetables, spinach, and sesame oil. Add the remaining soy sauce, sugar, sesame seeds, and optional rice vinegar. Toss everything together.
Serve: Garnish with additional sesame seeds and serve warm or at room temperature.

üëâ Pro Tip: For added flavor, drizzle more sesame oil on top just before serving!

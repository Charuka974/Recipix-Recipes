16. Tonkatsu (Japanese Breaded Pork Cutlet) üçñ

Ingredients:

For the Tonkatsu:

  1. 2 boneless pork loin or tenderloin chops (about 1 inch thick)
  2. Salt and pepper to taste
  3. 1 cup all-purpose flour
  4. 2 large eggs, beaten
  5. 2 cups panko breadcrumbs (Japanese breadcrumbs)
  6. Vegetable oil for frying

For the Tonkatsu Sauce:

  1. 4 tbsp ketchup
  2. 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  3. 1 tbsp soy sauce
  4. 1 tbsp mirin
  5. 1 tbsp sugar
  6. ¬Ω tsp Dijon mustard (optional)

Instructions:

Prepare Pork: Trim any excess fat from the pork chops and tenderize them by gently pounding them with a meat mallet. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
Dredge the Pork: First, dredge each piece of pork in flour, making sure to coat all sides evenly. Then dip it into the beaten eggs, letting any excess drip off. Finally, coat it with panko breadcrumbs, pressing gently to ensure an even coating.
Prepare Sauce: In a small bowl, mix together ketchup, Worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, mirin, sugar, and Dijon mustard (optional) to make the tonkatsu sauce. Stir until smooth and set aside.
Fry the Pork: Heat about 1 inch of vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot (about 350¬∞F / 175¬∞C), add the breaded pork chops. Fry each side for about 3-4 minutes until golden brown and crispy. If necessary, fry in batches to avoid overcrowding the pan.
Drain and Serve: Remove the pork from the oil and place it on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil. Let it rest for a few minutes before slicing.
Serve: Slice the tonkatsu into strips and serve with the homemade tonkatsu sauce on the side. It pairs wonderfully with steamed rice and shredded cabbage.

Ingredients:

For the Filling:

  1. 1 lb ground pork (or chicken, beef, or shrimp)
  2. 1 Â½ cups shredded cabbage
  3. 1 cup shredded carrots
  4. 2 tbsp soy sauce
  5. 1 tsp sesame oil
  6. 2 cloves garlic, minced
  7. 1 tsp ginger, minced
  8. 1 tsp rice vinegar
  9. 1 tbsp hoisin sauce (optional)
  10. 1 tbsp green onions, chopped (optional)
  11. Salt and pepper to taste

For the Egg Rolls:

  1. 12 egg roll wrappers
  2. Oil for frying (vegetable or canola oil)

Instructions:

Prepare Filling: In a pan over medium heat, cook the ground pork (or your chosen protein) until browned. Add garlic, ginger, and cook for 1-2 minutes. Stir in the shredded cabbage, carrots, soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and hoisin sauce (if using). Cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables soften and the filling is well combined. Let the mixture cool to room temperature. Optionally, stir in green onions.
Prepare Egg Rolls: Lay one egg roll wrapper on a flat surface. Place about 2-3 tablespoons of the filling near the corner of the wrapper. Fold the sides inward, then roll tightly from the bottom to the top, sealing the edge with a little water.
Heat Oil: Heat a large pot or deep skillet with about 2 inches of oil over medium-high heat.
Fry Egg Rolls: Carefully place the prepared egg rolls into the hot oil, a few at a time, without overcrowding. Fry for 4-5 minutes, turning occasionally, until golden brown and crispy. Use tongs to remove them from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
Serve: Serve the egg rolls hot with sweet and sour sauce, soy sauce, or your favorite dipping sauce.
